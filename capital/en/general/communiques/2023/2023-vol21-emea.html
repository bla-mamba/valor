Introducing Fractional Shares Trading
 in Canadian Stocks
and ETFs

Interactive Brokers recently expanded fractional shares trading to Canadian stocks and ETFs listed on the Toronto Stock Exchange and on CBOE Canada.

Fractional shares give investors a simple way to build a balanced portfolio and invest in shares regardless of the share price. With fractional shares, investors can divide investments among more stocks to achieve a better-diversified portfolio and put small cash balances to work quickly to maximize potential returns.

To trade eligible Canadian fractional shares, clients can use a cash quantity order that lets clients place orders in a specific cash amount. Fractional shares will automatically be bought or sold if the cash amount does not line up with a whole number of shares.

  • Log in to Portal
  • Click the User Profile (head/shoulders icon in the top right corner)
  • Click Settings
  • Click Trading Permissions
  • Under Stocks, click "Add" and then "Global (Trade in Fractions)"
  • Click Continue to enable

Once enabled, you can trade fractional shares from our IBKR Mobile, IBKR GlobalTrader, IMPACT App, Client Portal, IBKR Desktop or Trader Workstation (TWS) trading platforms. When placing an order, simply tap or click the quantity field to show the size wand, use the toggle to switch between dollars or fractional share quantity and enter the quantity for your order.

As an Interactive Brokers client, you are likely aware that client accounts receive credit interest on positive settled cash balances.1 After increases in interest rates by the US Federal Reserve, Interactive Brokers now pays up to 4.83% on positive settled USD cash balances.2

Fundamentals Explorer Now Includes a Securities Lending Dashboard

Use daily short sale data on thousands of securities worldwide to generate trade ideas, gauge short sentiment and evaluate your portfolio from a different angle.

The Securities Lending Dashboard lets clients evaluate short trades by accessing key securities lending metrics. Investors can access real-time metrics such as Utilization, Shares on Loan and Short Interest Indicators to gauge borrowing demand in a name, which may reflect market sentiment. They can also leverage historical data to view recent trends and compare activity across two tickers.

Trend Metrics include:

  • Utilization: The total quantity of the security on loan divided by the total inventory of the security. Expressed as percentage.
  • Lender Depth: A scale to indicate the number of lenders with an outstanding borrow in the name. 1: 0-5 lenders, 2: 6-10 lenders, 3: 11 or more lenders.
  • Average Duration: The volume weighted average duration of all open loans in the security based on Trade Date, expressed in calendar days.
  • Borrow Depth: A scale to indicate the number of borrowers with an outstanding borrow in the name. 1: 0-5 borrowers, 2: 6-10 borrowers, 3: 11 or more borrowers.
  • Days to Cover: for equity securities, the number of shares on loan in the security for the business date divided by the 30-day average daily trading volume in the cash market.
  • On Loan Quantity: Total quantity of the security on loan.
  • On Loan Value: Total value (USD) of the security on loan.
  • Short Interest Indicator: For equity securities, the number of shares on loan divided by the total number of shares in the public float, expressed as a percentage. For fixed income securities, the size on loan divided by the issuance size, expressed as a percentage.

Our Security Lending Dashboard is powered by complimentary and premium securities lending analytics from Orbisa, a leading market data provider. Gain transparency across your portfolio, develop strategic insight, validate sentiment and add depth to your trading analysis.

To use Fundamentals Explorer, log in to Client Portal and click the Research tab followed by Fundamentals Explorer.
